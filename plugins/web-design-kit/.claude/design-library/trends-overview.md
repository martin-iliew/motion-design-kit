# Design Library - Trends Overview
> Quick-load reference for pattern selection. Read this instead of opening every pattern folder.
> Machine-readable scoring lives in `catalog.yaml`. Full prose and snippets live in pattern folders.
> Last updated: 2026-03-13

---

## Hero

### Grid-Led Hero [hero | core 10/10]
- What: left-aligned copy, strong product visual, proof under the CTA, and calm spacing.
- Best for: SaaS, AI, enterprise, and developer-tool homepages.
- Feel: current, disciplined, and commercially serious.
- Avoid: centering everything or replacing product context with abstract art direction.

## Proof

### Sticky Proof Strip [proof | core 9/10]
- What: compact row of logos, metrics, or trust statements immediately after the primary CTA cluster.
- Best for: pages that need credibility fast.
- Feel: calm reassurance, not a giant logo graveyard.
- Avoid: oversized badge walls or repetitive trust clutter.

### Customer Story Metrics [case-study | core 9/10]
- What: short customer story with quantified outcomes and implementation context.
- Best for: mid-page trust building on serious product pages.
- Feel: specific and credible.
- Avoid: anonymous quotes with no measurable result.

## Product

### Product UI Showcase [product | core 9/10]
- What: large product frame plus concise explanatory callouts.
- Best for: product-led pages where the interface itself sells the story.
- Feel: concrete and high-signal.
- Avoid: decorative screenshots with no explanation.

## Features

### Modular Feature Grid [features | core 9/10]
- What: restrained card grid that groups features clearly.
- Best for: modern homepages that want a modular feel without novelty overload.
- Feel: current and structured.
- Avoid: bento-for-bento's-sake styling.

## Integrations

### Integration Logo Ecosystem [integrations | evergreen 8/10]
- What: ecosystem section combining partner logos with workflow-fit copy.
- Best for: platforms and tools that win on interoperability.
- Feel: practical, not decorative.
- Avoid: pure logo clouds with no meaning.

## Pricing

### Comparison Pricing Matrix [pricing | core 9/10]
- What: parallel plan comparison with visible differences and anchored CTA.
- Best for: pricing pages and homepage pricing previews.
- Feel: legible and low-friction.
- Avoid: hiding key differences behind accordions or microcopy.

## Social Proof

### Outcome Testimonial Wall [testimonial | evergreen 8/10]
- What: testimonials built around outcomes instead of admiration.
- Best for: pages with strong customer stories and recognizable identities.
- Feel: warm but evidence-based.
- Avoid: generic praise with no context.

## FAQ

### Objection-Handling FAQ [faq | evergreen 8/10]
- What: answers to buying, rollout, pricing, and trust objections.
- Best for: reducing final-scroll hesitation.
- Feel: direct and helpful.
- Avoid: support trivia that belongs in docs.

## CTA

### Risk-Reversal CTA Band [cta | evergreen 8/10]
- What: final CTA paired with reassurance like trial terms, onboarding help, or support.
- Best for: closing sections and pricing follow-through.
- Feel: decisive and low-friction.
- Avoid: decorative closers with no next-step clarity.

## Trust

### Security Compliance Block [security | evergreen 8/10]
- What: scannable trust section for governance, security, and procurement comfort.
- Best for: enterprise, AI, and developer-tool pages selling to serious teams.
- Feel: calm and credible.
- Avoid: tiny badge soups and compliance acronym dumps.
