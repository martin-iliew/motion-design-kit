# Design Rules

These are the non-negotiable design defaults for `web-design-kit`.

## Core stance

- Optimize for conversion, not novelty.
- Favor clear grids, readable type, predictable section rhythm, and explicit proof.
- Follow the visual language common on large product-company landing pages as of March 13, 2026.

## Hard rules

- Do not default to brutalism, collage, chaotic asymmetry, or editorial anti-layout patterns.
- Do not hide the primary CTA below the fold unless the user explicitly asks for a narrative hero.
- Do not ship a hero without product context, proof, or a concrete outcome statement.
- Do not make screenshots decorative only; product visuals must support comprehension.
- Do not rely on vague filler sections when the page is missing trust, pricing clarity, objections, or proof.
- Do not reduce contrast, body text size, or spacing discipline for aesthetic effect.

## Preferred patterns

- left-aligned copy with a strong visual anchor
- structured proof rails
- modular feature grids with restrained styling
- pricing that compares plans plainly
- customer stories with measurable outcomes
- CTA bands with risk-reversal or next-step clarity

## Restraint rules

- Use one expressive move per section, not three.
- If a grid is decorative, simplify it until it carries information.
- If a trend improves screenshots but weakens scan speed, reject it.
